Welcome to my personal website.

As a Ph.D. Candidate in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Cincinnati (UC), I conduct cutting-edge research in the Intelligent Autonomous Systems Research Laboratory (IASRL) supervised by Dr. Donghoon Kim. My primary dissertation research is on the development of a hardware-in-the-loop spacecraft simulator for on-orbit service missions, which involves designing, building, and testing a unique ground-based platform that mimics the dynamics and environment of space.

I am also involved in several other research projects that span across domains such as autonomous systems, explainable AI, sensor fusion, estimation, and uncertainty analysis. Some of the applications include collision-free multi-agent navigation, robust indoor positioning, dynamic wireless sensor networks, spherical unmanned aerial vehicles, and underactuated and fault-tolerant spacecraft control. I have published multiple papers in prestigious conferences and journals, and received two competitive fellowships from the Space Research Institute for Discovery and Exploration (SRIDE) and the Institute for Research in Sensing (IRiS) at UC.

I am passionate about advancing the frontiers of space exploration and discovery, and applying my skills and knowledge in aerospace engineering, electronics and communication engineering, and artificial neural networks to solve challenging and complex problems. I am eager to collaborate with other researchers and professionals who share my vision and enthusiasm for space robotics and sensing.
